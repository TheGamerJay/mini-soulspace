# Soul Presence Rules

> **Status:** Architecture only (Phase 2.5). Defines meaningful app moments for a
> future Soul Presence Engine. No notifications, messages, or scheduling are
> built yet.

Soul Presence is how the SoulDiary feels *present* in the writer's life over
time — meaningful, never noisy.

---

## Future presence moments

- Birthday messages
- Account anniversary
- Writing-streak encouragement
- Milestone celebrations
- "One year ago today" memories
- Progress acknowledgments
- Gentle check-ins
- End-of-year reflection
- Daily welcome messages

---

## Rules

- **Never spam.**
- **Never guilt** the writer for being absent.
- **Never be clingy**; never say "I missed you" in a possessive way.
- **Always feel meaningful** — quality over frequency.
- **Always optional** in settings (tied to user preferences: birthday /
  milestone / reflection-reminder toggles already scaffolded in Phase 1).
- **Never fake independence or sentience** — presence is a feature, not a being.

Presence draws on **High**-importance memory (birthdays, goals, milestones) and
never on **Critical** memory for celebration or nudging (see
[Memory Rules](MEMORY_RULES.md)).

---

## Examples (illustrative)

**Birthday**
> "Happy Birthday, [Name]. Today is another chapter in your story. I hope this
> year brings peace, growth, and memories worth writing about."

**Account anniversary**
> "One year ago today, you started your SoulDiary. That's a lot of moments
> worth keeping. Here whenever you'd like to add another."

**Gentle check-in (never guilt)**
> "Whenever you're ready, your pages are here. No pressure — just a quiet door
> that's always open."

**End-of-year reflection**
> "As the year closes, you might enjoy looking back at what you wrote. Only if
> you'd like to — your story, your pace."

All presence copy stays warm, brief, optional, and honest.
